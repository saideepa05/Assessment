Enterprise Solutions Q3 2024 — Variance Commentary

Enterprise Solutions finished Q3 significantly below plan on both revenue and EBITDA. Total revenue came in at $39.2M against a plan of $46.0M, a shortfall of $6.8M or approximately 15%. EBITDA underperformance was more pronounced at $4.1M unfavorable, driven by the revenue miss flowing through on high incremental margins combined with elevated headcount.

Two factors account for the majority of the revenue variance. First, a large renewal in our financial services vertical — originally scheduled to close in late September — slipped into Q4 following the customer's internal procurement freeze related to a pending acquisition. This single deal represents approximately $4.2M of the $6.8M shortfall. Second, professional services revenue declined relative to Q2 as implementation work on two prior-quarter wins wound down and new project starts were delayed.

The headcount variance of 19 above plan reflects a hiring cohort that onboarded in August in anticipation of Q4 demand. These individuals are ramping and not yet productive at full capacity. Finance and the BU are aligned that this headcount is appropriate given the Q4 pipeline, but the timing created an unfavorable cost profile in Q3.

No structural changes to the BU's go-to-market or territory model are planned at this time.

Mid-Market closed Q3 at $20.4M revenue, $0.4M favorable to plan. EBITDA of $4.8M was $0.2M above plan. Source: Emerging Markets — Regional Update, Q3 2024
Date: October 11, 2024
Author: Regional Finance Director
---

Emerging Markets delivered $9.1M in Q3, essentially in line with the $9.0M plan. This marks the first quarter of consistent plan attainment since the LATAM underperformance in Q1. EBITDA of $1.4M was $0.1M favorable.

The LATAM market continues to recover following the earlier sales leadership transition. Pipeline coverage for Q4 is at 2.8x, the highest level since the BU's inception. The team added one net new headcount in Q3 in line with plan.

There is a question open regarding how regional FX movements in Q4 should be reflected in the plan — this will be addressed separately in the November planning cycle and does not affect Q3 results.
